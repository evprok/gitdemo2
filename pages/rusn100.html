RUSN 100: Reading@Russia

The goal of this course is to explore reading as a social practice and text as an evolving unit of knowledge. We use folk, literary and visual texts from and about Russia, on the one hand, to develop students’ analytical close reading skills, on the other hand, to familiarize the students with the concept and tools of distant reading. Students will get practice with computational tools of digital humanities to analyze datasets ranging from digitized copies of literary texts to sets drawn from social media.

Learning objectives

  • ● Explore reading as a social practice and text as an evolving unit of knowledge
  • ● Analyze folk, literary, and visual texts from and about Russia in order to develop analytical close reading skills
  • ● Create, revise, and use Python programs in order to explore the concepts and tools of distant reading
  • ● Analyze datasets ranging from digitized literary texts to social media in order to learn computational tools and techniques used in the digital humanities
  • ● Evaluate the readability and efficacy of a website in order to practice reading and reflecting on what constitutes a text
  • ● Develop and present an original project that involves close and distant reading in order to communicate course ideas to a wider audience
