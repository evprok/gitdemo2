RUSN 380: Russian Cinema

This course will introduce students to Russo-Soviet cinema from its beginnings through the present. Films will be considered within socio-political, aesthetic and theoretical contexts. Students will learn about the major cultural periods of the twentieth-century Russia (pre-1917, NEP, Stalinism, Thaw, Stagnation, Perestroika, and the post-Soviet period) and discuss cinema as an intersection of political and cultural ideas of the time.

Learning objectives

  • provide an overview of Russian cultural history in the 20th and 21st centuries
  • introduce students to major Russian film genres and movements
  • provide students with the basic vocabulary of film studies
  • teach students to apply the appropriate method for visual and critical analyses
